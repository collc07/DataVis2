Suicide Deaths in Australia

The Suicide Deaths in Australia data is a dataset that shows the yearly number of suicide deaths broken down into various features.

In the 2020 edition, the Suicide Deaths data looks at various ways the suicide deaths are broken down in 2020 and some trends from 2011-2020, some of the ways the data is broken down are: Suicide Deaths By States, Indigenous and non-Indigenous Suicide Deaths, External causes of Suicide Deaths and Mechanisms of Suicide Deaths.

This visualisation aims to explore the different perspectives to see what they tell us about Suicide Deaths in Australia in 2020.

Suicide Deaths in Australian States and Territory (2011-2020)

All States except for Northern Territory had decreased in number of suicides from 2019 to 2020.

Queensland, New South Wales, and Victoria had the highest number of suicide deaths so they contributed the most to the suicide deaths in 2020.

The Gender and Age Group breakdown (2011-2020)


2020 had a lower death rate compared to 2019 despite the effects of Covid-19

Overall, the total number of suicide deaths has increased from 2011 to 2020, more specifically, the 20-24 years old age group. However, there is also an increase in mental health awareness which is a leading cause of suicide deaths throughout schoold and workplaces. The effect of this increase in awareness is shown through the decreasing rate of suicide deaths from 2019-2020.

Suicide continues to be the leading cause of death for 15-44 year olds and it also accounts for the highest number of years of potential life lost as a high proportion of suicides occur within the younger age groups.

Indigenous and non-Indigenous Breakdown


Indigenous deaths are highest in Queensland


The number of Indigenous deaths compared to non-Indigenous deaths is much smaller due to the different population sizes.

In 2020, Queensland has the highest number of Indigenous deaths at 310, while New South Wales had the highest number of non-Indigenous deaths at 4161.

Suicide is the second leading cause of death amongst Indigenous males whilst it is the tenth leading cause of death amongst non-Indigenous males. It is also the tenth leading cause of deaths amongst Indigenous females compared to it being the twenty-second cause of death amongst non-Indigenous females.

This is a cause of concern as this means many Indigenous deaths are premature and an increase in effort towards Indigenous mental health and awareness in needed.

Breakdown by External Causes of Death


Accidental death has the highest number of deaths under Suicide Deaths

Accidental death is the highest external cause of suicide deaths in 2020 with falls and other mechanisms of death making up a major part of this.

Breakdown by Mechanism of Death (2011-2020)


Poisoning by drugs remains as the highest mechanism of death



In terms of mechanism of death, Poisoning by drugs remains as the most used method for suicide deaths in Australia.

Poisoning by other forms has dropped from being the second leading mechanism of death in 2011 to the fifth leading cause of death in 2020 behind Other, Firearms and Falls.
